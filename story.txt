RogueWave Storyline
===================

The city used to breathe in light.
Neon District was loud, Acid Grid was alive, and Crimson Core powered every tower in the skyline.
Then the signal came: a silent wave from the deep lattice below the city.
Street drones failed first, then defense arrays, then the sky itself started to flicker.

You are one of the last Wavebreakers, an arena pilot wired to a reactive combat frame.
Your frame adapts during combat, learning from every close call, every shard, every level.
The more you survive, the stronger and stranger your build becomes.

The corrupted network does not send armies. It sends wardens.
At key breach points, the signal manifests as bosses:

- Shard Warden: a fast strike commander that anchors local corruption loops.
- Iron Tyrant: a siege engine wrapped in plated geometry and brute force.
- The Null Sovereign: the source entity, a reality-eating monarch buried in the core.

Your objective is simple to state and brutal to execute:
hold the arena, break each wave, cut down the wardens, and reach the core alive.

If Shard Warden falls, the first lock opens.
If Iron Tyrant falls, the city grid can breathe again.
If The Null Sovereign falls, the signal ends.

Every run writes another line in the same war:
Survive. Adapt. Overwhelm the wave.
